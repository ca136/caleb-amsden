About

Growing up on a small farm taught me two important lessons: knowing how to work hard and work smart. I call myself a Web Developer and a designer, and I spend most of my time building websites and apps.

Technical Background

I first start programming when my first boss gave me a old CentOS server and told me to build an API. I bought a book on Perl and then learned a lot about Linux, databases and scripting. In the next six months I went from unpaid intern to leading the technical team. Six months after we launched, we had already pulled in over $1 million in revenue and were profitable. I work relentlessly to build a successful product, and learned how to adjust to changing challenges and environments. Some of the challenges my team overcame include:

  • Scaling an API to deal with 1,000s of requests/second.
  • Creating an optimized database with over a 100 million records.
  • Building a system to sanitize new and existing records in real-time.
  • Creating an analytics platform to gain insights on data trends.

Full-stack developer, Experience designer

All my side projects have focused on building web apps that make people's lives easier (I have a soft spot for SMBs). This required becoming an expert on how to build interfaces that get out of the way and empower the user.

For my first full-stack web application I built a really useful app that helped sellers on Etsy easily create and send newsletters. The user experience was awful and 90% of my users used the app once. I learned from the mistake and built a new, clean UI that's converting at around 50%.

Most recently, Tech lead at a rising NYC-based startup

My current position is Lead Developer at I Like What You're Wearing. Forbes called us "Facebook + Vogue + Amazon". As the first technical hire at ILWYW, I inherited a 6K line backbone.js one-pager and an extremely slow 8K line django-piston API. Requests to our shop page were timing out after 30 seconds, and the whole ux was clunky and slow.

I converted the entire project to a standard site structure in Django only using Backbone to organize the pages with complex functionality. After refactoring, adding DB caching and AWS Cloudfront, we were able to reduce page load times to under 1 second for ever page on our site. I am also proud to have:

  • Designed and produced the entire front-end e-commerce platform. We received a lot of attention based on how we pushed the limits of a traditional fashion site while building something that our users loved.
  • Research, created and participated in pitches to investors. We were able to raise over $600K before we fully launched the product.
  • Built a very fast, easy-to-use and most of all reliable site: our only down-time occurred when our founder forgot to renew the domain.
  • Communicated effectively with team members by using data to help focus product vision and avoiding feature creep.
  • Created an amazing experience on mobile. By building a responsive site layout and optimizing for tablets, our tablet users purchased 20% more often than regular site users.
