About

Growing up on a small farm taught me two important lessons: knowing how to work hard and work smart. Now, I apply those lessons as a web developer, designer and entrepreneur.

Technical Background

I first start programming when my boss gave me an old CentOS server and told me to build an API. I bought a book on Perl and then learned a lot about Linux, databases and scripting. Six months after we launched, we had already earned $1 million in revenue and were profitable.

I worked relentlessly to build a successful product, and learned how to adjust to challenges and changing environments. Some of the challenges my team overcame include:

  • Scaling an API to deal with 1,000s of requests/second.
  • Creating an optimized database with over a 100 million records.
  • Building a system to sanitize new and existing records in real-time.
  • Creating an analytics platform to analyze data patterns.

Full-stack developer, UX designer

All my side projects have focused on building web apps that make people's lives easier (I have a soft spot for SMBs). This required becoming an expert on how to build interfaces that get out of the way and empower the user.

For my first full-stack web application I built a really useful app that helped sellers on Etsy easily create and send newsletters. The user experience wasn't very good and 90% of my users only used the app once. I learned from the mistake and built a new, clean UI that converts 50% of users that try the product.

Most recently, Tech lead at a rising NYC-based startup

Most recently I worked as Lead Developer at I Like What You're Wearing. Forbes called us "Facebook + Vogue + Amazon". As the first technical hire at ILWYW, I was responsible for crafting an experience that showcased the hottest new fashion designers.

I refactored the MVP, added DB caching and a CDN, and was able to reduce page load times to under 1 second for every page on the site. I am also proud to have:

  • Designed and produced the entire front-end e-commerce platform. Our users loved spending time on the site (over 6 min. average), and our designers were super passionate about how we added to their brand value.
  • Built a very fast, easy-to-use and most of all reliable site: our only down-time occurred when we forgot to renew our domain... Doh!
  • Created a great experience on mobile. By building a responsive site layout and optimizing for tablets, our tablet users purchased 20% more often than regular site users.
